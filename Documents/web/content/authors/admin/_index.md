## About Me

Welcome! I am a Ph.D. student in Quantitative Marketing at The University of Chicago Booth School of Business. Previously, I received my M.S. in Economics and Computation from Duke University. Prior to graduate school, I received a B.A. in Mathematics and a B.A. in Statistics (with honors) from the University of Wisconsin–Madison.

My research combines elements of consumer behavior analysis, econometric modeling, and practical applications in pricing and marketing strategy, offering valuable insights for businesses operating in competitive markets. I am also interested in the intersection of artificial intelligence and privacy, focusing on innovative methods that leverage AI technologies while ensuring the protection of personal data and upholding ethical standards in data usage.
